What if a game did not only test what you know, but showed how the world fits together? This is Webwoven, a game about finding paths through real, named connections.

In Single player, I pick an easy, normal, or hard route. I can leave the map open, or choose one of ten topics. Each round gives me a start, a goal, and a best distance. Now I find my own path.

Each card is a place I can go next. When I move, the map grows. It keeps my path and the choices I left behind. Three hints can help, but each costs points. Each card also explains its connection. Here, Lionel Messi connects to Rosario because he was born there. I can read the fact, see its picture, and check the source before I move.

Webwoven has three play modes. Single player lets me explore at my own speed. The Daily challenge gives everyone the same route and scoreboard. Multiplayer lets two to four people race on the same map. Players can leave and return without losing the room.

I started with a game idea, but no working product. I built the main product with Codex, using GPT-5.6 Sol. I gave Codex the goals, visual style, and play-test feedback. Codex helped build the app, server, data tools, tests, and public documents. It split large jobs into focused tasks for the web, server, data, security, and review. This saved time while keeping each part clear.

I kept using GPT-5.6 after the first draft. I played on desktop and phone, found problems, and gave them back to Codex. We made the play modes easier to find, removed unfair loops, improved the fact text, and rebuilt the phone map for touch. I made the final product, design, data, and release choices.

AI helped build Webwoven, but it does not play for you. During a round, there are no calls to AI or Wikidata. Rules, moves, hints, scores, and winners come from one checked local map. It contains three thousand nine hundred and seventy things and more than twenty-two thousand named connections.

Webwoven is live, works on desktop and mobile, needs no account, and is completely open-source. Connect anything, and discover why it is connected.
